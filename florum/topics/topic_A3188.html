England fans and the English media are unbearable during major tournaments.

The reason I (and I think the overwhelming majority of Man Utd fans) want rid of him now is because hes not worthy of a place in the team. But yes youre right theres also disappointment with him because he has so clearly failed to make the most of his potential. He also asked for a transfer in 2010, after which there were rumours his agent was talking to Man City. This led to United fans painting sign for City and youre dead on his house. He got a new improved contract with United after that but his relationship with most fans never recovered.

Hes about the same age as C. Ronaldo and joined the club at the same time. Rooney, however, was seen as the greater talent back then. In retrospect this seems ridiculous but thats how it was. So comparisons are still naturally drawn between the two: Rooney is seen as lazy for not making the most of himself the way Ronaldo has. Hes also known to hate going to the gym and likes his beer and chips.

He was also never quite consistent enough to nail down a single position, what this meant was when he was playing No 9 and he had a few bad games people said he should be No 10, and vice versa. Ferguson tried to make him into a rounded player able to operate behind another striker or off either wing. In retrospect he should have stayed a No 9. When United signed van Persie they probably should have sold Rooney.

Rooney was a talented player with a few glaring flaws in his game - like Stephen Gerrard he had no tactical disciple and tended to run around like a madman. Like Gerrard, he was able to come up with a few moments of magic that made people forget the fact that he essentially doesnt know how to play football (which is why he never made a convincing playmaker). Like with Gerrard, now his legs are gone hes no use to anyone.
